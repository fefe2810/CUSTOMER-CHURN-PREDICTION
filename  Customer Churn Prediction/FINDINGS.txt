CUSTOMER CHURN PREDICTION — FINDINGS SUMMARY
=============================================

PROJECT OVERVIEW
Predicted which telecom customers are likely to leave using classification models.
Dataset: Telco Customer Churn — 7,043 customers, 20 features.

WPS SHEETS FINDINGS
- Overall churn rate: 26.53% — roughly 1 in 4 customers left the service
- Month-to-month contracts had the highest churn at 2,220 customers
- Electronic check customers churned at 45.3% — nearly 3x higher than automatic payment methods
- Credit card automatic payments had the lowest churn rate at 15.3%

DATA PREPARATION
- Encoded all text columns to numbers using LabelEncoder
- Applied SMOTE to balance churned vs non-churned customers
- Final training dataset after SMOTE: 8,260 rows
- Test dataset: 2,066 rows

MODEL RESULTS
Logistic Regression:  Accuracy 81%  |  AUC 0.888
Random Forest:        Accuracy 84%  |  AUC 0.919
Random Forest was selected as the final model.

FEATURE IMPORTANCE
1. Contract — commitment level is the strongest churn signal
2. TotalCharges — reflects overall customer loyalty and tenure
3. MonthlyCharges — higher bills increase likelihood of leaving

KEY FINDINGS
- Random Forest correctly predicts churn 84% of the time
- Pricing and commitment level are the biggest drivers of churn
- Customers on month-to-month contracts paying high monthly charges
  are the highest risk group
- Locking customers into longer contracts significantly reduces churn
- Auto-payment setup strongly correlates with customer retention

BUSINESS RECOMMENDATION
Target high risk customers — month-to-month contracts with high monthly
charges paying via electronic check — with retention offers before they leave.
